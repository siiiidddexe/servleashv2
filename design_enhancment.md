Here are the UI/UX mistakes and fixes discussed in the video:

Avoid Emojis and Use Professional Icons (0:24-0:36):
Mistake: Using emojis in UI/UX can make an app look unprofessional and "vibe-coded."
Fix: Replace emojis with professional icons from libraries like Phosphor Icons or Lucide to instantly improve the visual appeal.
Don't Let AI Choose Colors (0:39-1:05):
Mistake: AI often selects bright, clashing colors that don't work well together.
Fix: Manually choose colors that complement each other. Incorporate color through relevant information and charts (e.g., micro charts) rather than relying on bright buttons and icons.
Avoid AI-Chosen Layouts (1:22-2:00):
Mistake: AI-generated layouts can be repetitive, busy, and inefficient, leading to unnecessary clicks and cluttered interfaces.
Fix:
Optimize Sidebar: Streamline sidebar navigation by ensuring only relevant links are visible, and tuck less frequently used options into popovers.
Professional Profile Cards: Replace generic gradient profile circles with more professional account cards.
Simplify Cards: Collapse multiple buttons into triple-dot menus, centralize dates, and use icons for chips to reduce visual clutter.
Improve Feature Layouts and Functionality (2:03-2:26):
Mistake: AI often creates sparse forms and misses essential features, leading to less user-friendly interactions.
Fix:
Use Modals for Forms: For sparse forms, use modals instead of flyouts to provide a more modern look and better organization.
Include Advanced Options: By default, collapse advanced options to keep the interface clean, but ensure they are easily accessible.
Add Missing Options: Implement crucial features like domain swapping or description fields to enhance user control and customization.
Refine Billing and Usage Pages (3:24-4:47):
Mistake: AI-generated billing pages often include useless cards and unorganized information.
Fix:
Remove Irrelevant Elements: Eliminate cards that don't serve a function.
Use Tabs for Organization: Create tabs to categorize information (e.g., "Usage," "Billing," "Plans").
Elevate KPIs: Instead of "vibe-coded" key performance indicators, use a simple two-column layout with small doughnut charts for better visual representation.
Streamline Pricing Plans: Reduce the number of pricing plans, adjust their hierarchy, and clearly show discounts and what each plan offers compared to the previous one.
Enhance Analytics Tab with User-Friendly Features (4:49-5:26):
Mistake: AI might create basic, unengaging charts and overlook simple, highly useful features.
Fix:
Interactive Data: Add toggles to compare individual links against each other.
Detailed Information in Rows: Include more useful information in rows with helpful icons to add visual interest and clarity.
Rich Visualizations: Replace basic bar charts with engaging visualizations like maps with shaded regions and corresponding data for a richer experience.
Optimize Landing Pages with Graphics (5:36-6:12):
Mistake: "Vibe-coded" landing pages often lack graphics, leading to a lack of trust and lower conversion rates.
Fix:
Incorporate Graphics: Add simple graphics, such as stylized link cards or slightly edited images of designed app pages (e.g., analytics or password protection screens), to establish trust and improve presentation.
Focus on Presentation: Landing pages prioritize presentation over complexity to achieve better conversion rates.
